# $700 all-in imaging kit — Evolution 9.25

Complete shopping list for **deep-sky + planetary** on the NexStar Evolution 9.25, including **f/6.3 focal reducer** and adapters.

**Strategy:** Buy a used **Canon EOS 80D** (best dual-purpose DSLR in budget) + Celestron imaging adapters new. **Keep your Sony NEX-6** for planetary video — it beats the 80D on planets and costs nothing extra if you only add an E-mount T-ring (~$25).

---

## Recommended: Canon 80D kit (~$680)

### Stack order (rear of scope → camera)

```
9.25" SCT rear cell
  → f/6.3 Focal Reducer/Corrector
  → SCT T-Adapter
  → Canon EF T-ring
  → Camera body
```

**Remove the focal reducer** for planetary sessions (native f/10, or add 2× Barlow later).

### Why 80D at this price

- **Deep sky:** RAW stills, good high ISO for a crop body, WiFi to phone
- **Planetary:** 1080p60 video for stacking (better than original 6D)
- **With f/6.3 reducer:** ~1487 mm effective focal length, f/6.3 — workable fields for M42, M31 on APS-C
- **Without reducer:** f/10 planetary / lunar at full resolution

## Used-market tips

- **80D body:** Shutter count &lt; 50k ideal; confirm WiFi works (menu → Wi-Fi function)
- **Reducer:** Buy **new** if possible — bad optics are costly to debug; used OK from reputable astro classifieds (~$100–120)
- **T-ring:** New cheap; threads must be smooth (strip risk on SCT thread stack)

| Item | Why later |

| Equatorial wedge | Long unguided DSO on alt-az; not required for bright targets |
